Information

I am currently Research Associate in Human-Robot Interaction at the University of Wisconsin Madison, working with Bilge Mutlu on a NASA ULI project to develop new methods for collaborative robotics in the context of aircraft manufacturing.

In my PhD, I researched and developed interactive machine learning systems for robots interacting socially with humans. I developed SPARC (Supervised Progressively Autonomous Robot Competencies), a framework of interaction allowing humans to teach robots to interact social in the real world.

Over summer 2019, I was working at ATR with a JSPS Summer Fellowship with Prof. Takayuki Kanda and Dr. Satoru Satake on robotic navigation in dense human environments.

Until April 2019, I was working as a Research Fellow at Plymouth University on the EU FP7 DREAM Project, which aimed to develop new Robot-Enhanced Therapies for children with Autism Spectrum Disorder.
